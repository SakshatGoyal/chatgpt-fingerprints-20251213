### 3. Which one fits your *large-table sketch* workflow?

1. **Budget-to-quality sweet-spot → Insta360 Link 2 + Arkon stand**  
   *4 K clarity, AI DeskView, nearly 30 in of vertical reach, all-in cost ≈ $270 (≈ 23 % below Reach).*  
   Lens pivots itself level, so you stay in flow instead of fiddling with rotation.

2. **Ultra-wide capture in tight spaces → IPEVO V4K Pro 120**  
   With a 120° lens it fits an **11 × 17 in** pad even on a short arm—a good choice if the camera sits *inside* the drawing area and you don’t want a tall pole.

3. **Use what you already have → Phone + Arkon**  
   Modern phones out-resolve the Reach, and the HD8RV29’s **29-in boom** clears most studio tables. Set “Do Not Disturb” and a manual-focus video app for a frictionless low-cost setup.

4. **Keep Reach but upgrade the head** (hybrid hack)  
   If you value the *one-hand glide* of Logitech’s arm, you can detach the StreamCam and attach a better webcam (¼-20 adapter needed). The arm accepts up to 0.5 kg comfortably.

5. **Money-no-object pro look → ZV-1 II + Elgato pole**  
   One-inch sensor leaps ahead in low-light and color depth; the 49-in pole spans drafting tables and brings the lens directly above architectural sheets. Only pick this if clients will notice the difference on 4 K review screens.

### 4. Quick setup tips for big-surface sharing

| Problem | Fast fix |
|---------|----------|
| Edge distortion on wide-angle cameras | Place the camera high enough (≥ 24 in) and enable manufacturer “DeskView / Keystone” correction where available. |
| Glare from desk lamps | Use *indirect* LED panels or clamp a $15 matte drafting board over glossy paper. |
| Shaky lines when you bump the table | Add a small sandbag (1–2 lb) over the Arkon or Reach base, or clamp the pole to the *side* of the desk rather than the top. |
| Need to zoom into details mid-call | Map a shortcut: **Insta360** gesture zoom, or **IPEVO Visualizer**’s 6× digital zoom. |

If you’d rather keep hardware minimal and value reach over AI tricks, the stand-plus-phone route is the most cost-efficient of all.
