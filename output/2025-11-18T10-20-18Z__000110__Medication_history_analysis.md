# **3. Why She Has Tremors ‚Äî A Plain Explanation**

There are two major possibilities for tremors caused by psychiatric medicines:

### **(A) Drug-Induced Parkinsonism**
- Happens when a medicine blocks movement circuits too strongly.
- Causes **shaking, stiffness, slowed movement**.
- Appears **weeks after dose changes**.
- Improves when the medicine is reduced or stopped.

### **(B) Tardive Movements**
- Happens after **months or years** of exposure.
- Movements can be jerky, twisting, repetitive.
- **Do NOT improve with Pacitane** (and Pacitane can make them worse).
- Sometimes remain even after stopping the medicine.

### **Which one does Suparna have?**

Based on the timeline:

- Tremors **exploded** only after **Risperidone and Paliperidone**.
- Tremors **reduced** after a **7-day washout**.
- Tremors got **worse** after injections.
- Pacitane only helped a little.
- Movements did **not** disappear fully even after stopping the offending drugs.

This means:

### **She most likely has a mix of:**
- **Drug-Induced Parkinsonism** (main part)
**plus possibly**
- **a Tardive component** (the part that did not fully go away)

That is exactly what her pattern suggests:
**A strong reaction to heavy dopamine-blocking medicines (Risperidone/Paliperidone).**
