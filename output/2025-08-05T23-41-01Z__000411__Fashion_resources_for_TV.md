### 3. DIY sleuthing tactics (cognitive-scientist meets investigative-journalist)

1. **Freeze-frame + reverse image search**
   Export a clear still, crop to just the shoe, and feed it into Google Lens or Pinterest Lens to surface visually similar products.

2. **Cross-reference costume credits**
   *The Sandman* costume designer Lucinda Wright often collaborates with British boot-maker Grenson—search “[Brand] + Lucinda Wright + boots” to spot patterns.

3. **Look for licensing partners**
   Netflix sometimes partners with fashion retailers; check the Netflix Shop and official press releases for “Inspired by *Sandman*” drops.

4. **Interrogate the silhouette**
   Note eyelet count, sole thickness, and toe shape. Plug those descriptors plus “side-zip” or “cap-toe” into specialty boot forums.

5. **Ask the hive mind smartly**
   When posting on Reddit, include: full-body screenshot, episode timestamp, any visible logos, and what you’ve ruled out. Crowd-sourcing works better when you show your homework.

### 4. Why footwear IDs lag behind jackets

Boots are often **custom-dyed or distressed** for continuity, and logos get black-taped for legal reasons. Jackets usually keep brand tags intact, making them easier to track via wardrobe sales or stylist Instagrams.
