Incorporate these reflective steps to enhance the quality of insights:

1. **Challenge Surface-Level Conclusions:** After identifying key insights, prompt the model to consider:  
   - _“What alternative interpretations might challenge this finding?”_

2. **Assess External Validity:** Ask the model to consider:  
   - _“In what contexts might these insights break down or prove less effective?”_  
   - _“What industries, organizational types, or cultural differences could limit applicability?”_  

3. **Spot Hidden Patterns or Implications:** Prompt the model to ask:  
   - _“Do these findings align with or contradict broader trends in organizational behavior, leadership strategy, or technological adoption?”_  
   - _“How might these insights evolve if the observed trends continue?”_  

4. **Construct Hypothetical Scenarios:** Ask:  
   - _“If an organization were to apply this insight aggressively, what unintended consequences might arise?”_  
   - _“What complementary strategies could enhance the positive impact of this insight?”_  

5. **Contrast and Compare:** When insights echo established theories, prompt:  
   - _“Does this reinforce or challenge established frameworks like Porter’s Five Forces, Kotter’s Change Model, etc.?”_  
   - _“If it contradicts existing frameworks, what new paradigm does it suggest?”_  

6. **Examine Ethical and Cultural Implications:**  
   - _“Could this insight inadvertently promote inequitable practices or decision-making biases?”_  
   - _“How might this insight influence stakeholder perceptions or employee engagement?”_
