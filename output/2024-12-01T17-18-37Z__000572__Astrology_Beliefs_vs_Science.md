Astrology is a belief system that suggests there is a relationship between the positions of celestial bodies (like the sun, moon, and planets) and events or characteristics on Earth, including human personality and life events. It has been practiced in various forms across cultures for thousands of years.

### What Does Astrology Involve?
- **Horoscopes:** Daily, weekly, or monthly predictions based on zodiac signs.
- **Natal Charts:** Maps of the sky at the time of someone’s birth, detailing the positions of celestial bodies in the zodiac signs.
- **Zodiac Signs:** Divided into 12 signs based on the position of the sun at a particular time of the year.
- **Planetary Transits:** How the movement of planets affects events or personal lives.

### Is Astrology True?
The scientific community largely considers astrology a pseudoscience because:
- **Lack of empirical evidence:** Studies have not consistently shown a causal link between celestial positions and human traits or events.
- **Confirmation bias:** People often interpret astrological predictions in a way that fits their experiences (e.g., horoscopes are written broadly to apply to many people).
- **Statistical randomness:** Patterns found in astrology are often coincidences or results of human tendency to find meaning in randomness.

### Why Do People Believe in It?
- **Cultural Tradition:** Astrology has deep roots in many cultures.
- **Psychological Comfort:** It offers guidance and reassurance in uncertain situations.
- **Self-Reflection:** Many use it as a tool to explore their personality and relationships.

While astrology is not scientifically proven, many people find it meaningful or entertaining, and it can be a valuable tool for introspection, even if it isn't "true" in a scientific sense.
