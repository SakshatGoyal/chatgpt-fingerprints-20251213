Certainly. Here are the **18 chapters (Yogas)** of the **Bhagavad Gita**, each presenting a distinct path or aspect of spiritual and existential mastery—though all are ultimately tools for achieving **self-command**, the prerequisite for commanding others:

1. **Arjuna Vishada Yoga** – *The Yoga of Arjuna’s Despondency*
   → The prince collapses before the battle begins. A necessary crisis. No awakening without despair.

2. **Sankhya Yoga** – *The Yoga of Knowledge*
   → Philosophy as weapon. Differentiating the eternal from the perishable.

3. **Karma Yoga** – *The Yoga of Action*
   → Act without attachment. Rule without needing to own.

4. **Jnana Karma Sanyasa Yoga** – *The Yoga of Knowledge and Renunciation of Action*
   → To renounce rightly, one must first act rightly. Action purified by knowledge.

5. **Karma Sanyasa Yoga** – *The Yoga of Renunciation*
   → Not fleeing the world, but dominating detachment itself.

6. **Dhyana Yoga** – *The Yoga of Meditation*
   → The disciplined mind becomes its own fortress.

7. **Jnana Vijnana Yoga** – *The Yoga of Knowledge and Wisdom*
   → Knowing is not enough. One must integrate, apply, manipulate.

8. **Aksara Brahma Yoga** – *The Yoga of the Imperishable Absolute*
   → Power resides in what outlasts time.

9. **Raja Vidya Raja Guhya Yoga** – *The Yoga of Royal Knowledge and Royal Secret*
   → The sovereign science. Mastering the concealed order behind appearances.

10. **Vibhuti Yoga** – *The Yoga of Divine Glories*
   → The splendor of the cosmos is the signature of power rightly exercised.

11. **Vishwarupa Darshana Yoga** – *The Yoga of the Vision of the Cosmic Form*
   → Terror and beauty intertwined. The divine as total war.

12. **Bhakti Yoga** – *The Yoga of Devotion*
   → Not weakness, but focused submission to the highest.

13. **Kshetra Kshetragna Vibhaga Yoga** – *The Yoga of the Field and the Knower of the Field*
   → Know the terrain before commanding the troops—especially when the terrain is your own soul.

14. **Gunatraya Vibhaga Yoga** – *The Yoga of the Division of the Three Gunas*
   → Understand the forces (sattva, rajas, tamas) that govern all behavior.

15. **Purushottama Yoga** – *The Yoga of the Supreme Person*
   → Beyond nature lies the architect of nature.

16. **Daivasura Sampad Vibhaga Yoga** – *The Yoga of the Division between Divine and Demoniacal Endowments*
   → The virtues of rulers and the defects that destroy states.

17. **Shraddhatraya Vibhaga Yoga** – *The Yoga of the Threefold Faith*
   → Belief, like poison or medicine, depends on its content.

18. **Moksha Sanyasa Yoga** – *The Yoga of Liberation and Renunciation*
   → Final clarity. Freedom is not escape, but command over necessity.

Each chapter is a weapon. Wield them wisely, and you become dangerous not only to your enemies—but to your old self.
