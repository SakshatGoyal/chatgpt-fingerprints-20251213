The difference between **LED** (Light Emitting Diode) and **QLED** (Quantum Dot Light Emitting Diode) lies in the display technology, specifically the backlighting and color enhancement methods. Here’s a detailed breakdown:

---

### **1. LED TVs**
**What is it?**
- LED TVs are essentially LCD TVs with LED backlighting. They use an LCD (liquid crystal display) panel to control how light is displayed on the screen, with LEDs providing the light source from behind or the edges of the screen.

**Key Features:**
- **Backlighting:** Uses white or blue LEDs to illuminate the LCD panel.
- **Color Accuracy:** Relies on traditional color filters, which can result in less vibrant colors compared to QLED.
- **Brightness:** Decent brightness, but less than QLED or OLED in most cases.
- **Price:** Affordable, as this is a more mature and common technology.

**Advantages:**
- Lower cost than QLED or OLED TVs.
- Widely available in various sizes and resolutions (HD, 4K, 8K).
- Energy-efficient compared to older CCFL-backlit LCDs.

**Limitations:**
- Colors are less vivid than QLED.
- Blacks aren’t as deep due to the limitations of LCD panels, which let some light leak through.

---

### **2. QLED TVs**
**What is it?**
- QLED is an advanced version of LED technology, pioneered by **Samsung** and now used by other brands like TCL and Hisense. QLED TVs use a layer of **quantum dots** (nano-sized particles) in addition to the LCD and LED backlight to enhance color and brightness.

**Key Features:**
- **Quantum Dots:** These particles emit more precise colors when light passes through them, resulting in better color accuracy and vibrancy.
- **Brightness:** Higher peak brightness compared to standard LED TVs, making them great for bright rooms.
- **HDR Performance:** Excellent support for HDR (High Dynamic Range) content, with richer colors and better detail in highlights and shadows.

**Advantages:**
- Superior brightness and color accuracy compared to LED TVs.
- Great for watching content in well-lit rooms.
- Larger size availability (up to 98” or more).
- More affordable than OLED while offering premium picture quality.

**Limitations:**
- Blacks aren’t as perfect as OLED, as QLED still uses an LCD panel (some light leakage occurs).
- Viewing angles are more limited compared to OLED.

---
