## 3️⃣ Sattvic & Temple-Style Buffet Experience (~20–35 min)

### 5. Sattvam (Banjara Hills, Road No. 2)

**Category:** Sattvic, no-onion-no-garlic vegetarian buffet (temple-style, pan-Indian)

- **Cuisine & authenticity:**
  - Sattvam is built explicitly on **sattvic cooking principles** – vegetarian buffet, no onion or garlic, moderate spice, emphasis on freshness and lightness.
  - Buffet includes a wide range of Indian regional dishes (koftas, bhindi, palak samosa, dal/rice options) plus some “videsi” touches like pizzas executed sattvic-style.
- **Health-conscious angle:** Very strong:
  - Focus on **low-toxicity ingredients**, minimal processed foods, no onion/garlic, gentler spice levels.
  - Reviewer notes highlight the relief of avoiding preservatives/food colours and even praise the **small, bite-sized dessert portions** to reduce waste and over-eating.
- **Ambience:** Banjara Hills, Road 2 – plush buffet layout, warm interiors, and considered one of the city’s more refined vegetarian buffets (above The Collective / opposite Park Hyatt).

**Distance & drive time**

- Distance from HITEC City to Banjara Hills Road 2 is ~13 km, with typical drive time estimates of about **20 minutes** in neutral traffic (can stretch closer to 30–35 min at peak).
- From My Home Bhooja, expect **~25–35 minutes**, still under your 40-minute preference most of the day.

**Rating**

- **Zomato:** 4.4 dining (447+ reviews).
- **Other platforms:** Magicpin & Justdial also show ratings ~4.4.

**Notable dishes / USPs**

- Sattvic paneer tikka, vegetable kebabs, palak samosa, mini-cookers of pulihora & lemon rice, large dessert and chaat spread in sattvic style.

Official Sattvam site, Zomato listing & reviews, Justdial rating page, SocialMaharaj’s detailed buffet review, Instagram buffet clips.
