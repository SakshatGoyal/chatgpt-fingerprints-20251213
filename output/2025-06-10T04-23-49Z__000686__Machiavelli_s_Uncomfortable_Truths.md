### Tone & Style – How he *delivers* hard truths

* **Blunt antithesis & aphorism** – “It is much safer to be *feared* than *loved*.”
* **Instruction by shock-value paradox** – Praises *merciful cruelty* (Prince Ch.17) or calculated deceit (Ch.18) to jolt readers away from moral habit.
* **Concrete historical exempla** – Rapid fire mini-stories of Borgia, Hannibal, Romulus embed the argument in gritty precedent, not theory.
* **Rhetorical frankness about the reader** – Opens Ch.15 promising the *verità effettuale* (“effectual truth”) rather than “imagined republics.” The reader is warned: comfort is not on offer.
