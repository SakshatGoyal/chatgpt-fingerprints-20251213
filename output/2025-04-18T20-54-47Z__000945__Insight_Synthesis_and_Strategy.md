Here's what I mean by an insight:

An insight is a revealing, non-obvious interpretation drawn from clearly observed or repeatedly validated themes. It reframes your understanding of a problem, behavior, or context by articulating why something is happening, often illuminating hidden needs, tensions, or motivations.

An effective insight:

- Clearly surfaces a nuanced understanding of underlying human or systemic truths.
- Provokes a shift in perspective, enabling fresh approaches to previously ambiguous or stubborn problems.
- Explains the "why" behind the data—the underlying forces or patterns driving the observed phenomena.

Example:

"Patients become anxious in waiting rooms primarily due to sensory isolation and informational uncertainty, rather than the wait duration alone."
