Making sticky rice with regular white rice is possible, but it won’t have the same texture as authentic sticky rice (like glutinous or sweet rice, used in traditional Asian dishes). Regular white rice lacks the same starch content, so while it can become a bit stickier, it won’t replicate the chewiness of sticky rice. Here’s how to make your regular white rice as sticky as possible:

---

### **Steps to Make Regular White Rice Sticky**  

1. **Rinse the Rice Thoroughly**:
   - Wash the rice in cold water 2–3 times to remove excess surface starch.  
   - If you want it stickier, skip this step (but it might turn out gummy instead of evenly sticky).

2. **Soak the Rice**:
   - Soak the rice in cold water for at least 30 minutes before cooking. This helps soften the grains and improves the texture.

3. **Use Less Water**:
   - Use slightly less water than usual when cooking (about 1.5 cups of water for every 1 cup of rice). Too much water can make the rice mushy rather than sticky.

4. **Cook the Rice**:
   - Cook the rice as usual in a rice cooker, on the stove, or in an Instant Pot.

5. **Steam It After Cooking (Optional)**:
   - If you want it stickier, after cooking, place the rice in a steamer lined with a damp cloth or parchment paper. Steam for 10–15 minutes. This step mimics how sticky rice is traditionally cooked.

6. **Let It Rest**:
   - After cooking, let the rice sit for 10 minutes with the lid on. This allows the steam to redistribute, making the rice softer and stickier.

---

### **For Authentic Sticky Rice Texture**  
If you’re looking for the exact chewy, sticky consistency, you’ll need **glutinous rice (sweet rice)**, often labeled as "sticky rice" at Asian grocery stores. It’s a specific variety of rice with a high starch content, perfect for dishes like Korean rice cakes, sushi, or Thai mango sticky rice.
