# What actually happened in August 2006
- **Dates & cause.** Surat flooded 7–10 Aug 2006 after very large releases from **Ukai Dam** on the Tapi; 80–95% of the city went under water. Peak spill was reported near **25,770 m³/s (~910,000 cusecs)**.
- **People & damages.** Official fatality counts are **~120–150** (some civil and media reports put it **up to ~500**). Estimated economic loss ranges roughly **₹9,500–₹21,000 crore** (~$2–4 B at the time). **Millions** were cut off without food/water for days.
- **How high did the water get (≈ “floors”)?** Contemporary and later accounts show high-water marks **~15 ft above ground** in places, with **~18–20 ft** reported in low-lying areas; many ground floors were inundated and people sheltered on **first floors**. # Counterfactuals (estimates with assumptions)

These are **scenario-style estimates**, not historical facts. Typical city-scale fatality rates in large floods range from **well under 1%** (good warning/low severity) up to **a few percent**; double-digit percentages are reserved for extreme, sudden, night-time failures in high-velocity zones.

**Baseline context for 2006 PAR.** Surat SMC population was **~2.9 million after its 2006 boundary extension** (metro ~3.6 million). Not all are in the floodplain; the 2006 event still affected the majority of households. For conservative math below, I’ll use **PAR ≈ 2.0–2.5 million** in the inundation footprint.

### Scenario B — Engineers hold, **dam overtops but still does not breach**

This assumes **poor warning** in some zones and **dangerous velocities** along channels, but **not** a structural breach. (This is illustrative; actual hydraulics require HEC-RAS modeling.)
