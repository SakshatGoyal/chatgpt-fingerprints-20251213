| **Creatine phosphate system** | For 5–10 seconds of max effort, the body uses stored phosphocreatine. | After that, it needs carbs—**not fat or ketones**—for sustained high output. |

- **Endurance athletes** (like ultra-runners) can thrive on keto, as their pace stays in the fat-burning zone.

This is where **targeted ketogenic diets (TKD)** come in: people on TKD consume **small amounts of carbs (e.g., 25–50g)** right before intense training. This provides **just enough glucose** for performance, while staying in ketosis the rest of the time.
