### 2. **What Is Corporate Strategy?**
- **Definition:** Long-term, organization-wide decisions that shape market position, industry role, and growth trajectory
- **Key Actors:** CEOs, Founders, Board Members, Chief Strategy Officers
- **Time Horizon:** Typically 5+ years
- **Focus Areas:**
  - Diversification and portfolio design
  - Mergers, acquisitions, and divestitures
  - Capital allocation across business units
  - ESG initiatives and macro-impact decisions
- **Key Signals to Look For:**
  - Moves that impact multiple divisions/business units
  - Industry entry/exit decisions
  - Resource deployment at a strategic scale

---

### 3. **Strategic Mindset & Context**
- **Mental Models:** Think in portfolios, ecosystems, and long-term value creation
- **Strategic Tensions:**
  - Balancing shareholder returns with environmental and social responsibilities
  - Navigating uncertainty while committing to high-stakes bets
  - Coordinating across decentralized or global business units
- **Organizational Impact:**
  - Sets the direction for all other strategies (business, functional, etc.)
  - Often catalyzes systemic change (e.g., digital transformation, new governance models)

---

### 4. **How Corporate Strategy Interacts with Other Strategy Types**
- **Corporate-Level vs. Business-Level:**
  - Business-level strategy is about **how to compete** in a market
  - Corporate strategy is about **where to compete** and **how to grow as an organization**
- **Corporate-Level vs. Innovation & Growth:**
  - Innovation may drive corporate change‚Äîbut corporate strategy often frames innovation investment as part of broader portfolio dynamics
