To avoid curdling or separation when using silken tofu in curry, follow these tips:

1. **Blend Thoroughly**: Blend the silken tofu until completely smooth and creamy before adding it to the curry. This helps it incorporate better and reduces the risk of separation.

2. **Add Tofu at the End**: Stir the blended tofu into the curry after removing it from direct heat. Residual heat is usually enough to warm the tofu without risking curdling.

3. **Lower the Heat**: If you need to simmer the curry after adding tofu, keep the heat very low. Avoid boiling the curry, as high heat can destabilize the tofu's protein structure.

4. **Thicken First**: If the curry needs to be reduced to thicken, do this step before adding the tofu. This way, you avoid prolonged cooking with tofu in the pot.

5. **Emulsify the Sauce**: Stir or whisk continuously as you incorporate the tofu. This can help the tofu blend evenly with the curry base, reducing the risk of separation.

6. **Balance Acidity and Salt**: High acidity or salt can increase the risk of curdling. If your curry is highly acidic (e.g., with tomatoes or tamarind), add the tofu carefully and mix well. Diluting the sauce slightly before adding tofu can also help.

7. **Consider a Starch**: Mix a small amount of cornstarch, tapioca starch, or arrowroot powder with the blended tofu before adding it. Starch can stabilize the tofu and reduce the chance of curdling.

By managing heat and incorporating the tofu with care, you can achieve a smooth, creamy curry without the risk of curdling.
