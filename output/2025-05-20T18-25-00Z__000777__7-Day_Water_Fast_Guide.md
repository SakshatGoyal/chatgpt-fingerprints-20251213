### How to read the table

* **Green-ish events** (opportunities) include autophagy, ketosis, growth-hormone pulses, improved insulin sensitivity, and possible mental clarity bursts.
* **Red-ish events** (risks) include electrolyte loss, orthostatic hypotension, muscle catabolism (stronger in the first 24–36 h for someone with little lean mass), uric-acid spikes, thyroid suppression, bradycardia, and dehydration—exacerbated by nicotine’s vasoconstriction.
* Your **smoking habit** slightly suppresses appetite but compounds circulatory stress (cold hands/feet, dizziness) and may mask low blood pressure.
* Being **“skinny-fat”** means glycogen and fat stores are adequate for energy, but the limited muscle reserves make early protein breakdown more consequential; resistance-type muscle engagement (isometrics, gentle band work) can blunt that loss.
* After **≈72 h** many report a “steady-state” phase: hunger is low, ketones >1.5 mmol/L fuel most of the brain, and mood often improves—but only if salt/potassium/magnesium are replaced.
* By **day 6** electrolyte debt is usually the limiting factor, not energy. Cramping, tingling fingers, palpitations, or confusion are yellow-to-red flags that you’re edging toward hyponatremia or hypokalemia.
* **Re-feeding at 168 h** must be slow (clear broth → soft-cooked vegetables → balanced solid meals over 48 h) to avoid re-feeding syndrome.

---

### Key evidence checkpoints

* **0-48 h:** transition from glycogen to ketones; autophagy initiates; protein breakdown peaks early and falls as ketones rise.
* **48 h:** higher parasympathetic activity and frontal-lobe slowdown—anger spikes, but set-shifting tasks may improve.
* **72 h+:** deeper ketosis, lower inflammatory markers, and (in animals/humans) stem-cell activation.
* **≥7 days:** recent human work shows muscle AMPK is not the main driver of protein loss; risk is chiefly electrolyte/volume-related.
* **Water-fast dangers:** muscle loss, arrhythmia, syncope, water intoxication if over-hydrated.

⚠️ **Medical disclaimer**
This timeline is educational and not a substitute for personalized medical supervision. A 7-day water fast—especially with nicotine use and low muscle reserves—carries non-trivial risks (syncope, severe electrolyte imbalance, cardiac arrhythmia). Consult a qualified clinician and consider supervised fasting or at minimum daily vitals and electrolyte checks.
