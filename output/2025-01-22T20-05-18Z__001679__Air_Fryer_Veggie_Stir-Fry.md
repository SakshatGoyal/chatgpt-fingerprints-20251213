### **Air-Fryer Veggie Stir-Fry with Flavorful Sauce**

#### **Ingredients:**
- **Vegetables**:
  - 1 cup cauliflower florets
  - 1 cup diced carrots
  - 1 cup diced capsicum (bell peppers, any color)
  - 1 cup diced zucchini or lauki (bottle gourd), optional
  - 1 small red onion, sliced
  - 1 cup diced green beans
  - 1/2 cup diced mushrooms (optional)
  
- **For the Marinade/Sauce**:
  - 2 tbsp olive oil
  - 2 tbsp soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)
  - 1 tbsp vinegar (white or apple cider)
  - 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup (optional for sweetness)
  - 1 tsp chili flakes (adjust to taste)
  - 1 tsp garlic paste or minced garlic
  - 1/2 tsp ginger paste
  - 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  - 1 tsp cumin powder
  - 1 tsp coriander powder
  - Salt to taste
  - A squeeze of fresh lemon juice (optional, for zing)
  - Freshly chopped coriander or green onions for garnish

#### **Instructions**:
1. **Prepare the Vegetables**:
   - Wash and chop all the vegetables into bite-sized pieces.
   - Pat them dry with a clean kitchen towel to remove excess moisture.

2. **Make the Sauce/Marinade**:
   - In a mixing bowl, combine olive oil, soy sauce, vinegar, honey/maple syrup, garlic, ginger, turmeric, cumin, coriander powder, chili flakes, and salt.
   - Mix well to create a thick marinade.

3. **Marinate the Vegetables**:
   - Add the chopped vegetables to the mixing bowl and toss them well to coat evenly with the marinade. Let them sit for about 5-10 minutes.

4. **Air-Fry the Vegetables**:
   - Preheat your air fryer to 180Â°C (350Â°F) for 2-3 minutes.
   - Spread the marinated vegetables in a single layer in the air fryer basket.
   - Air fry for 10-12 minutes, shaking the basket halfway through to ensure even cooking. If you prefer them crispier, cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.

5. **Serve**:
   - Transfer the air-fried vegetables to a serving bowl.
   - Garnish with freshly chopped coriander or green onions.
   - Optionally, sprinkle some sesame seeds or crushed peanuts for extra texture.

6. **Enjoy**:
   - Serve hot as a side dish, or pair it with steamed rice, quinoa, or noodles for a wholesome meal.
