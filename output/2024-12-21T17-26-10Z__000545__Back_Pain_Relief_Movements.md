---

### **1. Mechanism of Action**
- **Ibuprofen**: 
  - A non-selective NSAID, meaning it inhibits both COX-1 and COX-2 enzymes.
  - This broad action can reduce pain and inflammation but may also cause gastrointestinal (GI) side effects (e.g., ulcers, bleeding) because COX-1 helps protect the stomach lining.

- **Meloxicam**:
  - A selective NSAID that primarily inhibits COX-2 enzymes at lower doses.
  - This selectivity helps reduce pain and inflammation while being gentler on the stomach, but at higher doses, it can lose some of this selectivity.

---

### **2. Strength and Usage**
- **Ibuprofen**:
  - Available over-the-counter (OTC) in lower doses (e.g., 200–400 mg per dose) and by prescription for higher doses.
  - Best for short-term use and mild-to-moderate pain (e.g., headaches, menstrual pain, muscle aches).
  - Commonly used for acute conditions.

- **Meloxicam**:
  - Prescription-only medication, generally considered stronger than ibuprofen.
  - Used for chronic conditions such as osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis.
  - Its longer duration of action makes it more suited for once-daily dosing.

---

### **3. Onset and Duration**
- **Ibuprofen**:
  - Onset: Works quickly (30 minutes to 1 hour after ingestion).
  - Duration: Short-acting, lasting 4–6 hours, so it may require multiple doses per day.

- **Meloxicam**:
  - Onset: Slower onset (2–3 hours to reach full effect).
  - Duration: Long-acting, with effects lasting 24 hours, so it's typically taken once daily.

---

### **4. Gastrointestinal Side Effects**
- **Ibuprofen**:
  - Higher risk of GI side effects (e.g., stomach upset, ulcers) due to non-selective COX inhibition.
  - Often requires food or a proton-pump inhibitor (e.g., omeprazole) to reduce risks.

- **Meloxicam**:
  - Lower GI risk at standard doses because of COX-2 selectivity.
  - Still poses some GI risks at higher doses or with prolonged use.

---

### **5. Cardiovascular and Kidney Risks**
- **Both Ibuprofen and Meloxicam**:
  - Can increase the risk of cardiovascular events (heart attack, stroke) and kidney problems, especially in people with pre-existing conditions or at high doses.

- **Meloxicam**:
  - May have a slightly higher cardiovascular risk due to its longer duration of action.

---

### **6. Forms and Availability**
- **Ibuprofen**:
  - Available as tablets, liquid suspensions, and topical gels (OTC or prescription).
  - Widely accessible and inexpensive.

- **Meloxicam**:
  - Available only in prescription forms (tablets, capsules, liquid).
  - Less commonly used for acute pain due to its slower onset.

---

### **7. Ideal Use**
- **Ibuprofen**: Acute pain, fever, and inflammation for short-term relief (e.g., sports injuries, headaches).
- **Meloxicam**: Chronic pain and inflammation in conditions like osteoarthritis or rheumatoid arthritis.

---

### **Key Takeaway**
- **Choose ibuprofen** for fast relief of mild-to-moderate pain.
- **Use meloxicam** if you need long-lasting relief for chronic conditions, as prescribed by your doctor.
