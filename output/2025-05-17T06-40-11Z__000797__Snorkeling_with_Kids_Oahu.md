### How to decide (super-quick)

* **Closest to Honolulu:** Magic Island (<10 min) or Sans Souci (20 min).
* **Richest reef life + full services:** Hanauma Bay.
* **“Hotel pool” calm:** Ko Olina or Kuilima Cove.
* **Wanna stand on the sand *and* see fish from a boat?** Kāneʻohe Sandbar charter.
* **North-Shore summer day-trip:** Aweoweo for turtles, Shark’s Cove tide pools for splash-about.

### Safety & practical notes for a 7-year-old

* Fit a **child-size mask + dry-top snorkel** at a shop first; leaks ruin the fun.
* Use a **U.S. Coast Guard-approved kid’s life vest** even in lagoons; it keeps their head up while they focus on breathing.
* **Check daily surf reports** (hioceansafety.com) before any North-Shore or east-side outing; winter swells make shallow spots unsafe.
* Teach “**look, don’t touch**” – Hawaiian reef fish, turtles and corals are protected by law.
* Early morning = light wind, best visibility, and easier parking almost everywhere.

With this menu you can match mood, drive-time and your nephew’s comfort level without ever venturing into deep-sea water. Have a fantastic time exploring Oʻahu’s reefs together!
