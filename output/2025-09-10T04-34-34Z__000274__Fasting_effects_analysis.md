- Classic science on going without food shows the fuel switch: **glycogen → making new glucose → ketones (fat by-products)**. The **brain** uses more ketones over several days, easing the pull on blood sugar. **Strong research**  
- Early in a fast, your body **dumps extra water and salt** (you pee more). That makes the scale and “lean mass” drop look bigger than actual muscle loss. **Strong research**  
- **Autophagy** (“cell cleanup”) is well-shown in animals after 24–48 h, but exact timing and size of the effect **in humans** day-by-day is **uncertain**. I mark it **Speculative/Best estimate** day-to-day.  
- Where 10-day human water-only data are thin, I **extend** the 7-day patterns to days 8–10 and label those **Best estimate**.

> **Important note about “muscle loss”:** I give an **upper-bound “lean-tissue equivalent”** based on protein breakdown math. This **overestimates true muscle fiber loss** because a lot of the “lean drop” is **water, glycogen, and connective tissue**, not actual contractile muscle. The fact that **strength stayed intact at ~1 week** supports this.

# Day-by-day guide (Days 1–10)

### Day 1
- **Estimated fat lost:** ~0.10–0.20 kg (0.2–0.4 lb). **Best estimate**  
- **Estimated “lean-equivalent” lost (upper-bound):** ~0.50–0.63 kg (1.1–1.4 lb). **Best estimate**  
- **What’s happening:** You burn stored sugar (glycogen), start making new sugar, and **fat release** begins. **Strong research**  
- **Body systems:**  
  - **Brain:** still mostly runs on sugar; a tiny bit of ketones starts. **Strong research**  
  - **Kidneys:** you **pee out water and salt**—dizzy-on-standing can start. **Strong research**  
  - **Liver:** using up glycogen; starts making sugar from lactate/glycerol/amino acids. **Strong research**  
- **Good:** Early switch toward **fat use**. **Strong research**  
- **Watch-outs:** Light-headed when standing; don’t confuse water/“lean” drop with true muscle loss. **Strong research / Best estimate**  
- **Exercise today:** Easy walk/jog only; **no sprints or heavy sets**; drink water, mind salt loss. **Best estimate**  
- **Autophagy:** May **start rising**, but human timing isn’t nailed down. **Speculative**

## Big-picture upsides and drawbacks

**Upsides (adaptations)**
- Fast **switch to fat** as the main fuel; by around day 5, **~70%+** of resting energy is from fat. **Strong research**  
- **Brain** can use a lot of **ketones**, easing the need for sugar. **Strong research**  
- **Strength stays intact** at ~1 week; **aerobic top-end** drops. **Strong research**  
- In **modified fasts** (tiny calories allowed) under supervision, blood pressure, triglycerides, and blood sugar often **improve**. **Strong research** (note: not identical to water-only)

**Drawbacks / risks**
- **Dizzy on standing** from water/salt loss early on. **Strong research**  
- **Uric acid** rises with ketosis (gout risk if prone). **Strong research**  
- **Refeeding syndrome** risk if you eat too much too fast after—dangerous **electrolyte** shifts and **thiamine** needs. **Strong research**  
- **Gallbladder** flow slows with longer fasting → sludge/stones risk over time. **Strong research**  
- **Autophagy** stories are popular, but **human timing and size are unclear** day-to-day. **Speculative**

## Safe exercise rules for all 10 days (fits your light AM routine)
- **Keep it easy** (comfortable pace). **No HIIT, no max lifting.** If your heart rate drifts up unusually or you feel off, **back off**. **Best estimate backed by Strong research on capacity drop**  
- **What to do:** **Flat walking**, gentle mobility, nasal/diaphragm breathing. **Best estimate**  
- **Safety tips:** Avoid heat, stand up slowly, sit if dizzy. **Strong research for low blood pressure on standing**  
- **Stop and get help** if: repeated dizziness/fainting, racing heartbeat, chest pain, severe cramps/weakness, **cola-colored urine** (muscle breakdown worry). **Strong research (general safety)**  
- **After the fast:** **First 3–5 refeed days** are riskiest—**no hard training** until eating and symptoms are stable. **Strong research**

- **Total “lean-equivalent” lost (upper-bound):** about **3.6–4.7 kg (8–10 lb)**. Real **muscle fiber loss is likely much less** (strength stays intact at ~1 week). **Strong research to day 7; Best estimate to day 10**

## What we know vs. don’t know (limitations)
- Great modern **7-day, water-only** data exist; **10-day** is rarer, so days 8–10 are **Best estimates** based on day-7 patterns.  
- Many big studies (4–21 days) use **tiny-calorie “modified” fasts** with medical support; they show **safety and health marker gains** but aren’t identical to water-only.  
- **Autophagy:** animal data is clear; **human** day-by-day timing and size of the effect are **uncertain**.  
- Reported “lean loss” **overstates real muscle loss** because it includes **water/glycogen/connective tissue**. The **no-strength-loss at ~1 week** is the practical clue.

---

## Bottom line (for a 33-year-old, 220-lb man with light daily exercise)
Across 10 days without food, expect a **fast move to fat burning**, **brain using more ketones**, a **big water/“lean” drop early** with **modest true muscle loss**, **aerobic top-end down**, and **phase-specific risks** (early dizziness from water/salt loss, higher uric acid; the **biggest risk is when you start eating again**). Keep movement **easy and short**, avoid heat and hard efforts, and handle **refeeding slowly and carefully**.
