Quantitative

Quantitative risk assessment is the numerical side of risk management. It uses formulas to calculate potential losses in a specific monetary amount. SLE is the Single Loss Expectancy, or how much each incident is calculated to cost.  The ARO (Annual Rate of Occurrence) is how many times a year an incident is predicted to happen.   Annual Loss Expectancy (ALE) is the amount a company can expect to spend every year as a result of this particular threat.  The formula is ALE = SLE * ARO,
Here's a simplified example related to the Midtown Medical Center firewall breach:
Infographic showing the calculation of SLE x ARO = ALE using the information provided in the course text.
Assumptions:
Midtown Medical Center has 100,000 patient records.
Historical data suggests a breach could happen once every five years.
The estimated cost per breached patient record (including notification costs, legal fees, fines, and credit monitoring) is $150.
Calculations:
Single Loss Expectancy (SLE):
Calculate the cost of a single breach incident.
SLE = Cost per record * Number of records affected
SLE = $150 * 100,000
SLE = $15,000,000
Annualized Rate of Occurrence (ARO):
Estimate how often the breach is likely to happen per year.
ARO for a breach occurring once every five years = 1 / 5 = 0.2
Annualized Loss Expectancy (ALE):
Multiply the SLE by the ARO to get the expected cost per year.
ALE = SLE * ARO
ALE = $15,000,000 * 0.2
ALE = $3,000,000
Midtown Medical Center could potentially lose $3,000,000 every year, on average, due to patient record breaches. This figure helps the hospital decide how much to invest in improving their cybersecurity measures to lower the risk of future breaches.
